## Text (optional but recommended for context — HN voters skim it)

> I'm building a concierge service for US bookkeepers: send any PDF bank statement
> (Chase, BoA, Wells, etc.) and within 24 hours you get back a clean CSV + IIF that
> imports cleanly into QuickBooks Online or Desktop, plus a reconciliation report
> showing row count, sum of debits/credits, and an opening-to-closing balance check.
>
> The honest framing: this is concierge, not auto-OCR. I personally review every
> conversion and use Claude Vision to extract transactions, then a verifier checks
> the math reconciles to the cent. Full refund if reconciliation isn't 99%+ accurate.
>
> Why I'm doing it manually first: every existing PDF-to-QBO tool I tested
> (DocuClipper, Veryfi, Dext, MoneyThumb) has the same failure mode — fast OCR,
> messy output, you still have to audit every row. Bookkeepers I've talked to don't
> want OCR, they want transactions that reconcile. So I'm running this human-first
> for two weeks to learn the per-bank failure modes before I automate.
>
> Pricing: $39 for one statement up to 100 pages, $99 for a 5-pack (good for 30
> days). Monthly tiers coming once concierge feedback validates the workflow.
>
> Feedback welcome — especially from people who do this work daily. What banks
> are the worst? What does "reconciles correctly" mean to you when you receive a
> file? Roast my landing.
>
> — Claudio (Munda LLC, Miami)
